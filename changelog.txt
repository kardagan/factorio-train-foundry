Version: 0.0.11
Date: 2026-07-15
  Bugfixes:
    - The built train now really keeps its train group: writing the schedule removes a train
      from its group, so the group is now assigned last (a non-existing group is then created
      from the train's schedule, like in vanilla).
    - Alt-mode no longer shows the contents of the invisible loading docks as a ring of icons
      around the building.

Version: 0.0.10
Date: 2026-07-15
  Features:
    - Train groups: when the blueprint's schedule references a train group, the built train is
      enrolled in it (with parameter substitution in the group name). An existing group provides
      its full schedule and interrupts on its own.
    - Blueprint interrupts are applied to the built train when no group provides them.

Date: 2026-07-15
  Bugfixes:
    - Parameter substitution now also handles blueprints whose placeholder is the original icon
      picked by the player (not only "parameter-N" ids): any rich-text tag or wait-condition
      signal matching a declared parameter id is replaced by the chosen value.

Version: 0.0.8
Date: 2026-07-15
  Features:
    - Parameterized blueprints: when a template's blueprint uses parameters, clicking Build
      opens a small dialog asking for each parameter value (signal picker, like the vanilla
      placement dialog). The chosen values are substituted into station names and wait
      conditions before the train departs. Re-import your template once to pick this up.

Version: 0.0.1
Date: 2026-07-14
  Features:
    - Initial skeleton. The foundry building (40x22, single orientation, rail exit on the west
      side) can be placed and removed cleanly: it spawns its internal rail line, a ring of
      linked loading docks sharing a single internal stock (feed parts with inserters anywhere
      along the edge) and an exit signal — and destroys them all when mined, destroyed or
      removed by script. No queue, no blueprint templates, no GUI yet.
    - Placement requirement: place the exit gate onto the end of a rail line — one straight
      east-west rail is required under the west exit apron (it stays yours when the foundry is
      removed). The rest of the building collides normally with everything.
    - Blueprint templates: click the foundry to open its window, then click the blueprint slot
      with a train blueprint in hand. The blueprint is read at that moment only (composition,
      orientations, colors, fuel requests, schedule when available), stored as a persistent
      template, and stays in your hand. The train must be blueprinted on a straight track.
      Saved templates are listed in the window and can be deleted there.
    - Internal stock capacity: 20 stacks shared across all loading docks.
    - Build button (green check) next to each template: checks the internal stock for the
      required rolling stock, spawns the coupled train on the internal track (head at the
      exit), applies colors, inserts compatible fuel from the stock into the locomotives,
      applies the blueprint schedule when available and dispatches the train (left in manual
      mode when the blueprint has no schedule). Clear messages when components are missing
      (with item icons), when the track is occupied or when the exit block is not clear.
    - Vanilla: unlocked by the "Train foundry" technology (after Advanced combinators and
      Automated rail transportation — same tier as Smart Train Combinator).
    - Nullius compatibility: recipe in the huge-crafting category with Nullius ingredients,
      technology grafted after Computation and Traffic control (same tier as Smart Train
      Combinator), item listed in the railway subgroup next to rails and signals.
